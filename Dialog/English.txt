tmszzzzz_WhereMoonlightFalls_Gym_CrouchingClimbTitle= Crouching Climb
tmszzzzz_WhereMoonlightFalls_Gym_CrouchingClimb=
{anchor top}
[tmszzzzz]
Crouching climb lets you climb while staying crouched.
To perform this technique, you need to start with a crouch jump, {n}then continuously buffer climbs on the wall.
If you have downward speed, your crouch will be canceled.
This means the gap between releasing jump in each consecutive jump needs to be as short as possible.
If you are good at using two jump buttons, I recommend holding one of them, then tapping the other jump button to perform consecutive jumps.
Your dash has been disabled here. Try using crouching climb to cross this narrow gap.

tmszzzzz_WhereMoonlightFalls_Gym_CrouchingWallBoostTitle= Crouching Wall Boost
tmszzzzz_WhereMoonlightFalls_Gym_CrouchingWallBoost=
{anchor top}
[tmszzzzz]
Crouching wall boost means using neutral climbs during crouching climb, {n}then performing a wall boost at a specific position.
Wall boost has already been introduced in the Strawberry Jam Grandmaster Gym.
As an additional note, most failed wall boosts fail because no neutral climb was performed.
If you press left or right before climbing, you cannot perform a wall boost.
Therefore, for crouching wall boost, it is best to tap left and right lightly.
The leniency here is slightly low. This is only to limit the leniency when the wall boost is not performed.

tmszzzzz_WhereMoonlightFalls_Gym_SwapExtendedHyperTitle= Swap Extended Hyper
tmszzzzz_WhereMoonlightFalls_Gym_SwapExtendedHyper=
{anchor top}
[tmszzzzz]
Here is a swap block with a very short travel distance.
Intuitively, such a short distance should not allow an extended hyper at all.
However, although its travel distance is short, it moves faster than your dash.
This causes your dash movement to be completely covered by the movement of the swap block. It appears as if you dashed, but only moved a very short distance.
So when you stand on the swap block, you can perform a very lenient extended hyper. At the same time, you can also gain acceleration from the swap.

tmszzzzz_WhereMoonlightFalls_Gym_RetentionTitle= Bubble Retention
tmszzzzz_WhereMoonlightFalls_Gym_Retention=
{anchor top}
[tmszzzzz]
Retention is a general term for the phenomenon of blocked velocity being restored.
When you are blocked by an obstacle while carrying speed, and are no longer blocked a moment later, 
the speed you had before being blocked will be returned to you.
Combining retention with a bubble gives bubble retention.
Bubble retention is a technique with a very strange visual effect. Simply put, if you enter a bubble shortly after dashing past a wall corner, and there is no wall blocking the other side of the bubble,
then although you should have been pulled into the bubble, you are mysteriously given your forward speed back.
As a result, you slide out of the bubble in a strange way.
If you press dash again at this moment, you will be pulled back to the bubble's position and perform a bubble dash.
This allows you to perform a bubble dash while preserving horizontal speed.
In most places in this map, using a demodash to slide past the wall corner gives retention higher leniency. 
Do not worry. This technique does not make the inputs harder. It is just less intuitive.
In this example, you need to start with a hyper into an ultra, then jump and immediately use a demodash up-right.
If you do it correctly, you will slide out of the bubble. Then perform a down-right ultra.
If you do not slide out of the bubble, then you can only perform a diagonally down 45° bubble dash, which will hit the spikes.


tmszzzzz_WhereMoonlightFalls_Gym_BubbleCancelTitle= Bubble Cancel
tmszzzzz_WhereMoonlightFalls_Gym_BubbleCancel=
{anchor top}
I am not sure whether this technique has a commonly accepted name, so I will temporarily call it "bubble cancel".
When you touch entities such as a spring or a bumper while inside a bubble, it cancels your bubble state.
However, your next dash will still become a bubble dash.
This dash is no different from a normal dash in terms of input, but it will not trigger entities such as swaps that need to be triggered by a dash.
In this example, you need to use a bubble dash to pass through the swap ahead.
By the way, one bubble cancel in this map appears in a place combined with bubble retention. You need to touch the spring after sliding out of the bubble through retention.

tmszzzzz_WhereMoonlightFalls_Gym_MomentumCarryTitle= Delayed Momentum Carry
tmszzzzz_WhereMoonlightFalls_Gym_MomentumCarry=
{anchor top}
After you leave a moving block, or within 9 frames after that moving block stops, any jump or dash you perform will gain the momentum carried by that block.
The jump here does not have to be related to that block. A jump from any surface can receive the acceleration.
In this example, you need to step onto the moving block, then immediately dash up-left and wall kick after crossing the jump-through platform.
Strangely enough, this wall kick actually gains acceleration from the moving block, even though they seem completely unrelated.
By the way, one delayed acceleration in this map starts by grabbing the side of a moving block. 
This requires you not to release grab while dashing. Releasing grab will cause the 9-frame timer to start early.
